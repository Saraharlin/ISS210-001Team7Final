Women's Rights in the Workplace

Do you want to help fight to make the workplace a fair, safe, healthy, and enjoyable environment for women? Women's rights in general has been a long going fight. Between women's sufferage, independence from men, and many more historical and current events in the US and around the globe. However, women in the U.S have only been in the work force for less than a century. Previously, the workfore only consisted of men. Because of our patriachal society, the workplace is also a patriachal place. This makes women suffer many inequalities, harm, and disparity. To learn more, read below and also see what you can do to help the fight.

About

Women in the workplace are still being treated unequally.To this day women in the workplace still are treated differently as if they are not worth the same as a man. Women are still being paid less and many companies are male dominated. Working in some industries, women are not taken seriously and have to take an extra step to show or prove they are just as valuable as men. Every person deserves to be treated equally no matter their identity. Whether a person is identified as male or female and has the same skills and intelligence, they should be given the ability to pursue their expertise as it is just as useful and important. Companies have already taken action addressing this issue. The Treehouse Foods company is a packaging company that designs packages for private labeling for foods and beverages for retail grocery. Speaking with two professionals from the company that are both female have stated that they still see inequality between genders. That being said, their company has taken action and hosted a wide leadership meeting surrounding women giving them recognition. However, there are still many times that females are still being treated unequally meaning there is still much more room for improvement. Companies should look more into hosting wide leadership meetings and have groups and organizations so there are safe spaces to go to.

Breaking Through the Glass Ceiling

The glass ceiling is something affecting many women and minority groups all around the world today. The glass ceiling by definition is a barrier to advancement in a profession or career affecting minority groups. There are laws against discrimination along with recent efforts to promote diversity. However, according to Babic and Hansez, members of Human Resources Development Unit at University of Liege in Belgium state that women still remain the minority in decision-making positions. "In 2018, women occupied 16% of postions on the executive committees of large Belgian companies". They also state that women feel they recieve different treatment (Babic and Hansez). In order to change, we must continue to educate others, use our voices, expose wrongful treatment, and continue to vote for legislative change to protect women's rights in the work place.

Sexual Harrassment in the Workplace

The video above, "Sexual Harrassment in the Workplace", defines workplace sexual harrassment as,"Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature constitutes sexual harassment when submission to or rejection of this conduct explicitly or implicitly affects an individual’s employment, unreasonably interferes with an individual’s work performance or creates an intimidating, hostile or offensive work environment". While affecting men at a reported 43%, 81% of women have experienced a form of sexual harrassment in the workplace. There are forms of "hostile" and "quid pro quo" sexual harrassment further described in the video. This is something that women deal with very frequently in the workplace, and is often overlooked. Organizations are notorious for dealing with reportings of sexual harrassment fairly terribly. An important thing to note is there are things you can do to help. In order to prevent cases of sexual harrassment, help the victim, educate others on what sexual harrassment can look like, and call-out unprofessional and demeaning behaviors of others and businesses themselves.

Women In the work place

Click here to open this PDF in a new window


In the book Women in the Workplace: Wages, Respect and Equal Rights by Jeri Freedman, discusses several details on what it is like to be a woman in the workplace. She touches on many inequalities which women face in the workplace. She explains that pay between men and women are not equal. Furthermore, Freedman says that women are offered lower starting salaries than men. Not only does she talk about inequalities of pay but she also talks about what it feels like to be a woman in the workplace. Being a woman increases likelihood of vulnerability to sexual harassment. Sexual harassment is not just inappropriate touching, but also unwanted jokes and unwelcomed advances. She explains, that this occurs because of the way society perceives women today and throughout history..
